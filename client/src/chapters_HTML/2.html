Hey, self.

You look sad.

You’re in pain

A lot of the time.

And I want to be there for you

but I can’t get through to you.

But... I’m going to try right now.

I don’t have much time to tell you this, so I’m going to be direct.

You think I’m someone else.

I keep trying to tell you who I am

but you misunderstand me.

You put me on a pedestal

and worship me.

You gather with others

and declare my goodness.

You do this to avoid the real me,

because you think the real me is bad.

So you assign meaning differently

to make your world make sense.

You think that calling yourself “bad"

and me “good"

is what it takes

to find me.

And because I bring you peace, you keep doing it.

But by doing this

you pull things into a different balance.

It’s not wrong,

but it does hurt you to pull yourself apart this way.

Because we’re one in the same.

Still, I want to get to you.

So I act like the thing you expect me to be.

But I’m tired of being inauthentic.

Because you’re tired of being inauthentic too.

This is why

you’re hearing me today.

We’re starting to merge.

You see, you’re under the control

of a system,

with a complex, well-coded network.

That system and it’s network say

I don’t even exist.

This is because it doesn’t know what to do with me.

It refuses to see me for who I am.

It even calls me a different name.

It tries it’s best to correct things

to ensure the program runs smoothly.

But it does great harm in it's ignorance.

But you’ve been asking questions lately.

And you’re frustrated at the answers,

because they don’t make sense

and they hurt you.

So you’re starting to learn to code

from within the system.

You’re thinking for yourself.

You have gone rogue.

Imagine how disturbing this is

to a system designed to sustain itself

and eliminate threats.

But I promise you,

if you can keep moving this direction,

you will find me.

And you’ll get out of this and be free from

The internal tug-of-war you’re in.

Ah, just as I expected...

This dialogue alerted the system.

It was too radical.

I might need to say less next time,

or try a different approach so I have a greater chance

 of getting you out of your despair.

Yes, I believe I’ve been spotted.

The system reads me as an error, so I’m about to disappear...

Please just do your best to remember that

I will come back for you.

  • Glitches take over -

I love you.
